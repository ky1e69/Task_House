EMPLOYEE HANDBOOK

A. THE COMPANY

A.1. Company Profile: Who We Are and What We Do

We are a breed of industry-honed Virtual Assistants with wide ranges of experience in our respective domains or niches, capable of remotely executing a host of unparalleled services on various facets and levels of business.

We build trust with the clients whom we treat as our business partners. We operate on the fact that everyone is looking for the best virtual assistant services provider to help them cut overhead costs while achieving results. We are committed to making our client's business our business.

A.2. Mission and Vision

Mission
Taking on key operational tasks strategically for success-driven entrepreneurs, to give them significant advantage in fulfilling their goals and impact on their community.
Vision
To be reputable in fostering the vision of success-driven businesses through the use of internet technology, skilled professionals and outstanding business relationship.

A.3. Values

Commitment - We are committed to the company's goal of fulfilling every client's vision.
Order - We continuously strive for order in the way we design our processes.
Respect - We respect the laws, the government, and the people we work with.
Excellence - We achieve excellence by constant progress not perfection.

D. POLICIES

D.1. Hours of Work, Punctuality, and Attendance

D.1.1. Normal working hours shall be eight (8) hours a day or forty (40) hours a week. You are entitled to one (1) hour meal break and is not included in the calculation of the eight-hour work.

D.1.2. Considering the nature of the company's business, however, the management may require you to work during rest days and holiday if an emergency or the situation calls for it, this can be compensated with overtime pay or can be offset with regular working days as rest day in replacement of the consumed day for work whichever is approved by the management.

D.1.3. An employee who arrives one (1) minute or more after the specified start time is deemed tardy; an employee who leaves the work area before the shift ends is considered undertime.

D.1.4. Missed logs due to negligence shall constitute salary deduction and disciplinary action.

The following rules for salary deduction shall apply:
Failure to Clock In Null first four (4) hours of the shift or four (4) hours deduction
Failure to Clock out Null first four (4) hours of the shift or four (4) hours deduction
Failure to Clock in/out Null one (1) day shift or eight (8) hours deduction
The following rules for disciplinary action shall apply:
1st offense: Written Reprimand with warning of suspension
2nd offense: 2-5 days suspension
3rd offense: 5-10 days suspension
4th offense: Termination

D.2. Leave Policies

D.2.1. VACATION LEAVE

D.2.1.1. All regular employees are entitled to avail seven (7) days Vacation Leave and five (5) days Sick Leave every year. Newly regularized employees are entitled to the monthly earned leaves after regularization.

D.2.1.2. All leave credits refresh by January of the following year. Employees accrue sick leave at the rate of 0.4 days per month equivalent to 5 days for 1 calendar year. In the same manner, employees accrue vacation leave at the rate of 0.6 days per month equivalent to 7 days for 1 calendar year.

D.2.1.3. The Company shall pay the cash equivalent of the employee's unused Sick Leave credits at the end of the calendar year.

D.2.1.4. All Vacation Leave requests shall be filed two (2) weeks prior to the applicable date and must be pre-approved by either Management, Immediate Supervisors, or Delegators

D.2.2. SICK LEAVE

D.2.2.1. Employees with excused absences because of illness or injury must inform his/her immediate Supervisor or Delegators on the day of his absence through any reasonable means for the latter to make certain adjustments if need be.

D.2.2.2. Upon return to work, the employee must file the Sick Leave form so his/her absence is properly documented.

D.2.2.3. The employee must provide a medical certificate and provide a proof of physician's care for absences of two (2) or more days.

*The medical certificate requirement is temporarily waived due to the continuing danger of the pandemic, which makes it impossible to attend hospitals and clinics for check-ups.

D.2.3. EMERGENCY LEAVE

D.2.3.1. Employees may take Emergency Leave and take time off work immediately due to unforeseen circumstances.

D.2.3.2. In all cases, the right to leave will be limited to a reasonable amount of time, which will not exceed two working days.

D.2.3.3. Employees must notify their immediate Supervisor, Delegator, or Human Resources as soon as reasonably possible about the reason for their absence and agree on a return date.

D.2.4. MATERNITY LEAVE

Under the Expanded Maternity Leave under R.A. No. 11210, the expectant female employee is entitled to:

  • Maternity leave period of one hundred and five (105) days with pay
  • Option to extend for an additional thirty (30) days without pay
  • Extension of fifteen (15) days for solo mothers
  • Additional days for child-birth related purposes

E. PERFORMANCE EVALUATION

E.1. Performance evaluation shall be conducted every six (6) months or as deemed necessary by the management.

E.2. The evaluation shall be conducted by the immediate supervisor and shall be reviewed by the management.

E.3. The evaluation shall be based on the following criteria:

  • Quality of Work
  • Quantity of Work
  • Job Knowledge
  • Initiative
  • Reliability
  • Attendance
  • Cooperation
  • Attitude

F. TERMINATION OF EMPLOYMENT

F.1. Resignation

F.1.1. An employee who wishes to resign must submit a written notice to their immediate supervisor at least thirty (30) days prior to the effective date of resignation.

F.1.2. The notice period may be shortened or waived at the discretion of the management.

F.1.3. The employee must complete all clearance requirements before their last day of work.

F.2. Company-Initiated Termination

F.2.1. The company may terminate employment for just causes as provided by law, including but not limited to:

  • Serious misconduct or willful disobedience
  • Gross and habitual neglect of duties
  • Fraud or willful breach of trust
  • Commission of a crime or offense
  • Similar causes as provided under Article 282 of the Labor Code

G. CLEARANCE PROCEDURES

The following must be completed before the release of final pay:

  • Return of all company property including but not limited to:
    • Laptop and accessories
    • Mobile devices
    • Access cards
    • Other company-issued equipment
  • Settlement of all financial obligations
  • Transfer of all work-related documents and files
  • Exit interview with HR
  • Signed clearance form from all concerned departments

H. DATA PRIVACY

H.1. By accepting employment with the company, employees consent to:

  • Collection and processing of personal information for employment purposes
  • Storage and maintenance of employment records
  • Sharing of necessary information with government agencies and other authorized entities

H.2. The company commits to:

  • Protect employee personal information in accordance with the Data Privacy Act of 2012
  • Use information only for legitimate business purposes
  • Maintain confidentiality of all employee data
  • Provide access to personal information upon request

I. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY RIGHTS

I.1. All work products created during employment belong exclusively to the company, including but not limited to:

  • Software code and applications
  • Designs and artwork
  • Documentation and written materials
  • Innovations and improvements
  • Patents, copyrights, and trademarks

I.2. Employees shall:

  • Assign all rights to intellectual property created during employment to the company
  • Assist in securing and protecting the company's intellectual property rights
  • Not use company intellectual property for personal gain

J. USE OF FACILITIES AND RESOURCES

J.1. Company resources must be used responsibly and for business purposes only.

J.2. The company reserves the right to monitor:

  • Email communications
  • Internet usage
  • Computer and network activities
  • Phone calls and messages

J.3. Prohibited activities include:

  • Installing unauthorized software
  • Accessing inappropriate content
  • Using company resources for personal business
  • Sharing access credentials

K. OUTSIDE EMPLOYMENT

K.1. During employment with the company:

  • Employees must not engage in any other employment or business activities without prior written approval from management
  • Any approved outside employment must not:
    • Conflict with regular work hours
    • Affect job performance
    • Create a conflict of interest
    • Compete with company business

K.2. Violation of this policy may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination.

L. NON-COMPETE CLAUSE

L.1. For a period of three (3) years following the termination of employment, employees shall not:

  • Directly or indirectly engage in any business that competes with the company
  • Work for or provide services to any competitor within the Philippines
  • Start a competing business
  • Assist others in competing business activities

L.2. This restriction applies to:

  • Similar products and services
  • Same target market and customer base
  • Geographic areas where the company operates

L.3. Legal action may be taken for violation of this clause.

M. NON-SOLICITATION

M.1. For three (3) years after employment ends, employees shall not:

  • Solicit or attempt to solicit any company clients or customers
  • Encourage any client or customer to terminate their relationship with the company
  • Recruit or attempt to recruit company employees
  • Encourage any employee to leave their employment with the company

M.2. This includes both direct and indirect solicitation through:

  • Personal contact
  • Electronic communications
  • Social media
  • Third parties

N. CONFIDENTIALITY

N.1. Confidential Information includes but is not limited to:

  • Trade secrets and proprietary information
  • Client and customer data
  • Financial information
  • Business strategies and plans
  • Technical specifications and source code
  • Employee information
  • Marketing strategies
  • Pricing information

N.2. Employees must:

  • Maintain strict confidentiality during and after employment
  • Not disclose information to unauthorized persons
  • Take reasonable precautions to prevent unauthorized access
  • Return all confidential materials upon termination

N.3. Breach of confidentiality may result in:

  • Immediate termination
  • Legal action
  • Claims for damages

O. RULES OF CONDUCT

O.1. General Conduct

  • Maintain professional behavior at all times
  • Treat colleagues with respect and courtesy
  • Follow company policies and procedures
  • Maintain regular attendance and punctuality
  • Perform duties with diligence and efficiency

O.2. Prohibited Conduct

  • Dishonesty or fraud
  • Insubordination
  • Harassment or discrimination
  • Violation of safety rules
  • Unauthorized disclosure of confidential information
  • Misuse of company resources
  • Reporting to work under the influence of alcohol or drugs

O.3. Progressive Discipline

Violations of company policies may result in:

1. Verbal warning
2. Written warning
3. Suspension
4. Termination

*The company reserves the right to skip steps in the progressive discipline process depending on the severity of the offense.
