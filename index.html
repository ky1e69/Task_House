PROBATIONARY EMPLOYMENT & EVALUATION

All new hires undergo a probationary period‚Äîa time for both the employee and the company to assess long-term fit.

Duration

The probationary period typically spans six (6) months from the employee's official start date. During this time, employees are expected to:

Learn the company's culture, processes, and expectations
Demonstrate competence and character aligned with company values
Meet performance standards as communicated by team leads or account managers

Evaluation Timeline

First 30 days: Coaching and orientation progress
Third month: Mid-probation check-in and feedback session
Fifth to sixth month: Final evaluation for regularization eligibility

Failure to meet training or performance standards, or accumulating any of the following during probation, may lead to early termination:

Absences exceeding 5% of total training days
More than three (3) instances of tardiness
Repeated behavioral or performance violations

Important Notes

Leave during probation is considered Leave Without Pay
Probationary employees are expected to align quickly with team values, protocols, and attendance standards
